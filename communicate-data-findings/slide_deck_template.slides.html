PISA Dataset Visual Exploration¶

Isaac Paulson¶

Investigation Overview¶

How do assessment scores compare across countries? Genders?

Dataset Overview¶

PISA is the Programme for International Student Assessment. PISA consists of a triennial survey of 15-year-old students.

The PISA database contains the full set of responses from individual students, school principals and parents. Data for this analysis is from the 2012 PISA cycle.

Visualization 1 - Distribution of Student Scores¶

The first step of the analysis is to check the distribution of student scores for math, reading, and science. They all look normally distributed.

Visualization 2 - Scores by Gender, Ranked by Country¶

These plots show the mean and range scores by gender, ranked by countries with the highest scores.

Shanghai scored the highest in all subject areas, with Hong Kong and Singapore not far behind. Singapore is second in math, Hong Kong is second in the other subject areas, and vice verse.

How are the genders scoring compared to each other?¶

In math and science, males are doing about the same or better than females in most countries. In reading, females scored higher in all countries!

Conclusions - Next Steps¶

In general, females are not scoring high in reading and low in math and science.

More investigation will be necessary.

We could look for correlations between reading scores and other variables such as what classes students are taking and attitudes towards reading. We might have to look in other years of the PISA cycle for such variables on reading.
